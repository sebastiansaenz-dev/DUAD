Planets

Mars

Known as the "Red Planet" due to iron oxide on its surface, Mars is a cold, desert world with a thin atmosphere. It is the focus of intense scientific study due to its potential for having hosted liquid water and life in the distant past.

  • Aproximate distance from the sun: 227.9 million km
  • Rotation time: 24.6 hours
  • Number of moons: 2
  • Presence of atmosphere: Yes (thin, mostly carbon diaoxide)

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a massive gas giant composed mostly of hydrogen and helium. It is famous for its iconic Great Red Spot, a colossal storm that has been raging for centuries.

  • Aproximate distance from the sun: 778.5 million km
  • Rotation time: 9.9 hours
  • Number of moons: 95
  • Presence of atmosphere: Yes (thin, mostly hydrogen and helium)

Saturn

Saturn is best known for its spectacular and complex ring system made of ice and rock particles. As the second-largest gas giant, it possesses a low density and a powerful magnetic field that generates stunning auroras at its poles.

  • Aproximate distance from the sun: 1.4 billion km
  • Rotation time: 10.7 hours
  • Number of moons: 146
  • Presence of atmosphere: Yes (thin, mostly hydrogen and helium)
