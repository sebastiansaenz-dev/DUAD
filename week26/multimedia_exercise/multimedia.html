Animals

Bear

A bear is a large, heavy mammal in the family Ursidae, known for its shaggy coat, stocky legs, and short tail. There are eight living species, including polar bears, grizzly bears, and giant pandas.

  • Weight: 80kg - 600kg
  • Length: 1.7m - 2.5m
  • Main Diet: Fruits, berries, roots, insects, fish and small mammals
  • Lifespan: 20 - 30 years

Lion

The lion is a social big cat that lives in groups called prides, characterized by distinct sexual dimorphism. It is an iconic apex predator of the African savannas, often referred to as the "king" of the jungle.

  • Weight: 150kg - 250kg
  • Length: 1.7m - 2.5m
  • Main Diet: Zebras, antelopes, buffalo and other ungulates
  • Lifespan: 10 - 14 years in the wild

Shark

Sharks are cartilaginous fish defined by their flexible skeletons and exposed gills. They possess highly developed senses, such as electroreception, making them highly efficient marine predators.

  • Weight: 50kg - 2000kg
  • Length: 1m - 6m
  • Main Diet: Fish, crustaceans, mollusks, seals and plankton
  • Lifespan: 20 - 70 years
